The Gap We're Closing

Three Failures Costing Tanzania Tourism Billions

Before SafariGo, every international booking carried three unresolved risks that neither side could fully escape.

🔒
Transactional Trust Deficit

B2C: International tourists wire thousands to unverified operators with no escrow, no verification trail, and no arbitration if services fail. B2B: Local operators are equally unprotected — last-minute tourist cancellations and foreign credit network chargebacks after services are already rendered leave operators with zero recourse.

💸
Payment Fragmentation & Liquidity Crisis

A severe integration gap persists between global credit rails (Visa, Mastercard, Amex) and local mobile wallets (M-Pesa, Tigo Pesa, Airtel Money). Legacy escrow models hold 100% of funds until trip conclusion — paralyzing operators who need upfront capital for non-refundable TANAPA/TAWA permits and lodge deposits weeks before the tourist even arrives.

📉
Revenue Leakage & Data Blindness

Vast percentages of safari bookings occur informally over WhatsApp — outside any audited framework — causing substantial state revenue leakage through uncollected VAT and Tourism Levies. Simultaneously, state ministries and wildlife authorities lack a single source of truth for real-time tourism data, leaving policy formation and strategic investment planning operating in the dark.
